Bio

Born in 1989, I received a B.S. from Olin College of Engineering, Needham Massachusetts, in 2012—designing my own major in robotics engineering. In the summer of 2012 I joined the Florida Institute for Human and Machine Cognition, and participated in the DARPA virtual robotics challenge—which we won! Since the fall of 2013, I've been with the Human Centered Robotics Lab at the University of Texas at Austin, pursuing a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering. Since 2015 I've been a NASA Space Technology Research Fellow, collaborating with Johnson Space Center's Robotics Team. I've studied legged locomotion, the control of compliant robots, and frequency domain system identification—all of it working towards the problem of planning and controlling movement in mechanically complex systems.
